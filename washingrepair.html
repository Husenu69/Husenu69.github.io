Common Repairs

Washer problems we fix every day

From minor issues to major failures — here's what we handle across all brands and washer types.

💧

Washer Won't Drain

Standing water after the cycle is often caused by a clogged drain pump, blocked drain hose, faulty lid switch, or pump motor failure. We clear the blockage and replace failing components.

🔄

Washer Won't Spin

Clothes soaking wet at the end of the cycle? Common causes include a broken drive belt, worn motor coupler, faulty lid switch, or control board issues. We identify and fix the exact failure point.

⚡

Washer Won't Start

If your washer doesn't power on or respond to controls, it may be a failed door latch, control board, thermal fuse, or incoming power issue. We run a full electrical and mechanical diagnostic.

🌊

Washing Machine Leaking Water

Leaks can come from a damaged door gasket (front-load), cracked hoses, faulty water inlet valves, loose connections, or a worn tub seal. Fast repair prevents water damage to flooring and walls.

📳

Excessive Vibration or Shaking

Violent shaking during the spin cycle usually means worn shock absorbers, broken suspension rods, leveling issues, or a damaged drum bearing. We restore smooth, balanced operation.

🔊

Loud or Unusual Noises

Banging, grinding, squealing, or clicking sounds often indicate drum bearing failure, a foreign object in the drum, worn belt, or motor issues. We perform a full noise diagnostic.

🚫

Won't Fill with Water

If your washer isn't filling, the problem is usually a faulty water inlet valve, clogged inlet screens, a defective pressure switch, or a broken timer. We test and replace the right part.

🖥️

Error Codes & Control Board Issues

Modern washers display error codes for specific faults. We decode the error, diagnose the root cause, and repair or replace control boards, sensors, and related electronics.

🌡️

Not Heating Water Properly

If your washer's hot cycle runs cold, the heating element, thermistor, or water temperature sensor may be faulty. We test and replace the component responsible.

🔒

Door or Lid Won't Lock / Open

A faulty door latch or lid lock prevents the cycle from starting — or traps laundry inside. We replace door lock assemblies quickly so you're back in business.

🤢

Musty Smell or Mold

Front-load washers are prone to mold buildup in the door gasket and drum. We clean, inspect, and replace gaskets as needed — and show you how to keep it from returning.

⏱️

Cycle Takes Too Long

An unusually long wash cycle often points to a faulty water inlet valve, pressure switch, or control board that keeps the machine stuck in one phase. We get your cycle times back to normal.

Warning Signs

Is your washer trying to tell you something?

These symptoms often appear before a major failure. Addressing them early usually means a smaller repair bill.

  • Clothes are still soaking wet after a full cycle
  • Water remains in the drum after the cycle ends
  • Washer shakes violently or "walks" across the floor
  • Grinding, banging, or squealing sounds during operation
  • Water pooling on the floor around the machine
  • Musty or mold smell from the drum or door seal
  • Error codes appearing on the display
  • Washer won't start, pause, or respond to controls
  • Cycle times are much longer than normal
  • Hot water cycle runs cold
  • Drum rotates but clothes aren't being agitated
  • Door or lid won't open at end of cycle

Preventive Care

Keep your washer running longer

Simple habits that extend appliance life and reduce the chance of breakdowns.

  1. Clean the door gasket and drum monthly — especially on front-load machines — to prevent mold and odor buildup.
  2. Use the correct amount of HE detergent. Excess suds damage pumps and leave residue on components.
  3. Leave the door ajar after each cycle to allow the drum and gasket to dry out.
  4. Check pockets before loading — coins, keys, and small items damage the drum and pump.
  5. Don't overload the washer. Overloading strains bearings, belts, and the motor.
  6. Clean the lint trap and drain pump filter every 1–3 months to prevent drain clogs.
  7. Check and tighten inlet hose connections every year to prevent slow leaks.
  8. Ensure the washer is level — unlevel machines wear out shock absorbers and bearings prematurely.
