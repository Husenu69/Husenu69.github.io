Dryer broken? Don't just replace it.

A dryer that won't heat, won't turn, or takes three cycles to dry one load isn't just an inconvenience — in some cases, it can be a safety hazard. Lint-clogged vents and failing heating components are among the leading causes of household fires in the United States.

At Zoltan Appliance Repair, we provide professional dryer repair throughout Williamsburg, Virginia. Whether your dryer is electric or gas, a stack unit or freestanding, a basic model or a high-tech smart appliance — we diagnose the problem correctly and fix it right the first time.

🔥

Dryer fires are more common than most people think

The U.S. Fire Administration reports that dryers cause thousands of house fires every year, with clogged venting as the leading cause. If your dryer is taking longer than normal to dry clothes, schedule a service call — it may be a safety issue, not just a performance problem.

Common Repairs

Dryer problems we fix every day

From no heat to no power — here's what we handle across all electric and gas dryer brands.

🔥

Dryer Not Heating

The most common dryer complaint. On electric dryers, we check the heating element, thermal fuse, thermostats, and cycling thermostat. On gas dryers, we test the igniter, gas valve solenoids, flame sensor, and radiant sensor. We identify the exact failure — not just replace parts at random.

⚡

Dryer Won't Start

If your dryer is completely unresponsive, the problem may be a blown thermal fuse, failed door switch, broken start switch, faulty control board, or a power supply issue. We run a full electrical diagnostic to find the root cause.

🔄

Drum Won't Spin

A dryer that hums but doesn't tumble typically has a broken drive belt, worn idler pulley, or failed drum motor. We inspect the full drum drive system and replace worn components to restore smooth rotation.

⏱️

Takes Too Long to Dry

If clothes need two or three cycles to dry fully, the most likely culprits are a clogged exhaust vent, failing heating element, blocked lint trap, or a worn moisture sensor. This issue can also be a fire hazard — don't ignore it.

🔊

Loud Squealing or Grinding

Squealing is usually worn drum bearing rollers or a failing idler pulley. Grinding often means damaged drum glides, a broken drum seal, or debris caught between the drum and cabinet. Both get worse fast — repair early.

🌡️

Overheating

A dryer that gets too hot, scorches clothes, or shuts off mid-cycle is typically suffering from a clogged vent duct, failed cycling thermostat, or faulty high-limit thermostat. Overheating is both a performance and fire-safety issue.

💨

No Airflow / Vent Issues

Restricted airflow from a clogged lint trap, crushed duct, or blocked exterior vent cap causes overheating and poor drying performance. We inspect and clear the full vent path from the dryer to the exterior exhaust.

🖥️

Error Codes & Control Board

Modern dryers display fault codes for specific component failures. We decode the error, diagnose the root cause, and repair or replace the control board, sensors, or related electronics.

🚪

Door Won't Latch or Stay Closed

A broken door latch, worn door strike, or damaged hinge prevents the cycle from running. We replace the door latch assembly quickly so you're not holding the door shut while it runs.

📳

Dryer Vibrates or Shakes

Unusual vibration is often caused by worn drum rollers, a misaligned drum, or an unleveled machine. We inspect the support system and restore smooth, quiet operation.

💡

Drum Light or Display Not Working

Burned-out drum lights or unresponsive displays can indicate control board issues or simple component failure. We test and replace the right part without guesswork.

🌀

Wrinkled or Damp Clothes After Full Cycle

If clothes come out wrinkled or still damp, the moisture sensor bars may be coated with fabric softener residue, or the timer or control board isn't ending the cycle correctly. We clean and calibrate the sensor system.

Warning Signs

Is your dryer trying to tell you something?

These symptoms often appear before a major — or dangerous — failure. Don't ignore them.

  • Dryer runs but produces no heat
  • Clothes take two or more cycles to dry
  • Dryer shuts off before the cycle ends
  • Exterior of dryer gets unusually hot to the touch
  • Squealing, grinding, or thumping sounds during operation
  • Burning smell while dryer is running
  • Drum rotates but clothes don't tumble properly
  • Dryer won't start or respond to controls
  • Error codes appearing on the display
  • Door won't latch or stay closed
  • Clothes come out wrinkled or still damp after full cycle
  • Lint accumulating around the dryer or exhaust vent

Preventive Care

Keep your dryer running safely and efficiently

Simple maintenance habits that extend dryer life and, more importantly, reduce the risk of fire.

  1. Clean the lint trap before every single load — a clogged lint filter restricts airflow and forces the heating system to work harder.
  2. Inspect and clean the exhaust vent duct at least once a year. Hire a professional if the duct run is long or has multiple bends.
  3. Check that the exterior vent cap opens freely when the dryer is running and isn't blocked by debris or nesting animals.
  4. Avoid overloading the drum — oversized loads take longer to dry, strain the motor and belt, and leave clothes wrinkled.
  5. Clean moisture sensor bars every few months using a cotton ball and rubbing alcohol. Fabric softener residue coats the sensors and causes inaccurate drying times.
  6. Make sure the dryer is level. An unlevel machine causes excessive vibration that wears out drum rollers and bearings prematurely.
  7. Use rigid or semi-rigid metal duct instead of flexible plastic or foil accordion duct — plastic ducts crush, trap lint, and are a fire risk.
  8. Never run the dryer when you're asleep or away from home — most dryer fires start unattended.
